Meet the Marsupials

Kangaroos

Habitat: Kangaroos live in grasslands and open forests across Australia.

Movement: They can hop up to 35 mph and jump 25 feet in a single bound.

Social Life: Kangaroos live in groups called mobs that can have over 50 members.

Baby Joeys: Newborn joeys are only 2 cm long and climb into their mom's pouch to grow.

Koalas

Diet: Koalas only eat eucalyptus leaves and need to eat 200 to 500 grams daily.

Sleep: They sleep 18 to 22 hours per day to save energy from their low nutrition diet.

Habitat: Koalas live in eucalyptus forests along Australia's eastern coast.

Special Skill: They have two thumbs on each hand to help them grip tree branches perfectly.

Quokkas

Fame: Quokkas are called "the world's happiest animal" because they always look like they're smiling.

Home: Most quokkas live on Rottnest Island near Perth, Australia.

Social Nature: They're very friendly and curious, often approaching humans for photos.

Size: Quokkas are about the size of a house cat and are excellent climbers.

Marsupial Facts

Kangaroos: Males can grow up to 6 feet tall and have powerful hind legs for jumping. Koalas: They have thick, woolly fur and large noses to help them smell the best eucalyptus leaves. Quokkas: They're small and round with short tails and tiny ears that make them look adorable.

Kangaroos: They're herbivores that graze on grass and can go long periods without water. Koalas: They're picky eaters and will only eat certain types of eucalyptus leaves from specific trees. Quokkas: They eat leaves, stems, and bark, and are most active during the cool hours of dawn and dusk.

Kangaroos: They can't walk backwards and are featured on the Australian coat of arms. Koalas: Baby koalas eat their mother's special poop to get the bacteria needed to digest eucalyptus! Quokkas: They became internet famous for taking "selfies" with tourists and are protected by law in Australia.
